Description: Reports to: Head Preparator Department: Collections Management Classification: Non-exempt, full-time Start Date: Early September 2018 DMA Mission: The Dallas Museum of Art is a space of wonder and discovery where art comes alive. The DMA will: Place art and our diverse communities at the center around which all activities radiate. Pursue excellence in collecting and programming, present works of art across cultures and time, and be a driving force in contemporary art. Strengthen our position as a prominent, innovative institution, expanding the meaning and possibilities of learning and creativity. Scope of Position: The Preparator is responsible for art handling, installation, preparation and packing/unpacking of the permanent collection, exhibitions and loans. This position may require travel as a courier for loans, exhibitions, or for special packing/unpacking, installations/deinstallations of works of art. The Preparator projects a positive and professional image of the Museum, representing the division to colleagues and the external community. A strong candidate for this position will have a deep appreciation of the mission and aspirations of the DMA and the capability to articulate the DMA’s mission, values, achievements, potential, priorities to internal and external audiences. Essential Functions: Handle works of art in various situations according to DMA guidelines and standard museum practices Install, deinstall works of art Pack and unpack works of art, following provided instructions, or improving them for the safety of the objects, in coordination with the Registrar assigned to each project Move works of art safely within storage areas and throughout the building Assist photography and conservation departments in moving art during treatment and/or photography Act as Lead Preparator for exhibitions as assigned Receive/release works of art Assist with loading/unloading shipments from vans/trucks Secure each shipment in coordination with Registrar assigned to each project Maintain off-site and on-site art storage areas and art preparation areas, keeping all tools and supplies secure and in stock Maintain strict confidentiality of all DMA projects, personnel issues, shipments and art movement schedules Responsible for documentation of location change after each art move Prepare works of art for loan, installation or storage as scheduled by registrars or conservator including, but not limited to attention to existing frames, felt linings or spacers, glaze, backing boards, special mounts, and retrofit crates Assist with local pickups or deliveries using DMA art van Assist with inventory process as needed Other duties as assigned. Requirements: Education, Experience, and Certifications: Three years of experience as professional art handler, preferably in a museum setting with prior matting and framing responsibilities Education equivalent to Bachelor’s degree in Art History or Fine Arts Any combination of acceptable training and experience Excellent speaking/understanding of the English language including, art history terms and techniques Skill to safely work on all installation equipment including ladders, forklift, and occasionally a high lift or on scaffolding Ability to visually inspect artwork Ability to sit or stand for long periods of time, lift, push, pull heavy, fragile, odd-shaped objects carefully Ability to carry 50 lbs Ability to safely employ the use of a variety of tools, equipment and to handle over sized and/or heavy art objects and exhibition material Ability to follow instructions and work cooperatively with a team Microsoft Office Suite skills required Knowledge and experience in collections data base systems preferred Ability to speak a second language - preferrably Spanish The Dallas Museum of Art is committed to building a culturally diverse staff and is an Equal Opportunity Employer.
