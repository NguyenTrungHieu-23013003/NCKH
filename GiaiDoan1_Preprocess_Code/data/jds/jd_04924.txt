Heluna Health invites applications for the position of Distance Learning Specialist within the Center for Learning and Innovation (CLI), a branch within the Population Health Division at the San Francisco Department of Public Health. CLI is dedicated to fostering a culture of learning, trust, and innovation. It organizes several programs focused on 1) internal training and workforce development; 2) recruitment of the future public health workforce through meaningful internships; 3) catalyzing innovations in public health through human centered design principles; and 4) capacity building for external public health agencies and related stakeholders. Under the direction of the CLI Manager for Capacity Building Initiatives, this position will play a key role in getSFcba, a project funded by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention to provide to state, local and territorial health departments training and technical assistance in several high impact HIV prevention strategies including HIV testing, pre-exposure prophylaxis or PrEP, and syringe service programs. getSFcba maintains peer-to-peer learning collaboratives to help jurisdictions share experiences and lessons learned in implementing these and other strategies. Collaboratives use several distance learning strategies such as an online portal to support asynchronous communication and web-based video-conferencing to support paritcipants’ quality improvement efforts. getSFcba is also charged with hosting webinars, designing and deploying eLearning modules, and providing technical assistance around digital health programs, such as the use to two way text messaging applications to support patient adherence to PrEP. The Distance Learning Specialist will work with the CBA Manager and others at CLI to use a wide range of digital learning strategies effectively. The Specialist’s primary responsibilities include creating training materials including eLearning modules as well as developing other interactive teaching media as well as manuals, power point presentations, and other teaching aids. The Specialist also will work closely with subject matter experts to convene learning collaboratives in person and online using web-based videoconferencing software. The Specialist also will be responsible for maintaining peer-to-peer learning collaboratives utilizing Groupsite’s web-based portal and maintain a repository of online learning resources (e.g., videos, toolkits, lectures). In addition, the Specialist will serve as an administrator for CLI’s Learning Management System, built in WordPress, which facilitates online learning and exchange between staff from health departments and community partners. This position also will assist with hosting webinars and will provide technical assistance to internal and external partners who seek to implement digital learning. Finally, this position will collaborate with the overall grant team to assist with evaluation, reporting efforts, coordination of messages, and the highest standards for interactive learning. This is a temporary, grant-funded, Full time, benefited position. Employment is provided by Heluna Health.
