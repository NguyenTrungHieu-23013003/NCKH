Overview At Minco, you can make a difference by being part of something that is evolving, growing and continually improving. Minco products can be found everywhere from on an oil rig mile under the sea to satellites miles above our heads - and in your doctor’s office! Our temperature sensing, controlling, and heating, as well as the flexible circuits that pull it all together, are in products that save people’s lives, explore the world, protect our military, and bring energy to everyone. Do you enjoy troubleshooting problems and utilizing computer systems to enter and track information? Then the Material Coordinator position in our Flex Production Planning product family is for you! Every day you will help Minco succeed by maximizing your team’s performance while living Minco’s _icare_ values - Integrity, Communication, Accountability, Respect, & Energy. Responsibilities Reviews, verifies, and enters incoming and outgoing orders, materials, and transportation with proper documentation Ensures that work requirements are completed appropriately with accurate information Troubleshoots discrepancies and receives assistance on complex problems Performs a variety of operational projects and audits records, such as cycle counting, opening and closing shop orders, working with returned materials, processing international, and operating the ERP Assists with responding to daily questions and concerns from warehousing, inventory, production, customer service, or other functions Performs a variety of shipping, receiving, and material duties Ensures compliance with operating policies and procedures to maintain a safe environment and efficient operations Communicates with maintenance, quality control, production, and other departments to maintain a safe and effective environment and to ensure quality operations Assists with maintaining a clean and safe working environment and may perform various janitorial or housekeeping tasks Performs other duties as assigned Qualifications Experience Requirements Two years of experience in a warehouse, shipping, or material environment Physical Requirements Positions in this class typically require: learning, reading, concentrating, thinking, communicating, stooping, kneeling, crouching, reaching, standing, walking, pushing, pulling, lifting, typing, grasping, feeling, talking, hearing, seeing, and repetitive motions. Very Heavy Work: Exerting in excess of 100 pounds of force occasionally and/or in excess of 50 pounds of force frequently, and/or in excess of 20 pounds of force constantly to move objects Work Environment Will be subject to noise, fumes, mechanical parts, electrical currents, chemicals, and other environmental factors in manufacturing and logistic environments Skill Requirements: Understanding of documents and forms related to the shipping process Understanding of practices related to cycle counting, opening and closing orders, returned materials, and related processes Operating within an enterprise resource planning environment Serving as a specialist in one or more of the shipping, receiving, or materials processes Troubleshooting basic problems or data Handling and labeling hazardous materials based on area of assignment Ensuring compliance with safety, environmental, and operational policies and procedures PC skills; Microsoft Office applications (Word, Excel, PowerPoint) Communicating with co-workers to provide and receive direction
