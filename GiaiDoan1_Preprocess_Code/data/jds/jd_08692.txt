The Computer History Museum (www.computerhistory.org), located in the heart of Silicon Valley, California, seeks a technically experienced and highly motivated individual for the position of Web Designer/Developer. The Museum's mission is to preserve and present for posterity the artifacts and stories of the information age. This unique and exciting institution is home to one of the largest collections of computer-related artifacts in the world; Backed by leaders and innovators of the computer industry, the Museum brings computing history to life through its speaker series, exhibitions, oral histories and website. The Web Designer/Developer will work alongside the museum's IT team to help maintain and improve the museum's website and overall web architecture. The best candidates have a strong understanding of industry trends and are proactive in keeping with industry standards. An understanding of layout aesthetics and a rigor for programming are crucial. An understanding of the entire web development process, including design, development and deployment, with back-end knowledge, is important. Responsibilities The Web Designer/Developer is primarily responsible for maintaining the museum's website. He or she will design and build web pages based on requests from various museum teams, which may range from vague concepts to strict, pre- determined specs. The Developer should be flexible enough to handle both situations, and should be able to clearly communicate with others to finalize designs. When not creating new pages, the Designer/Developer will perform routine website maintenance, which includes updating older web pages and online exhibits. These older pages may utilize outdated/obsolete technologies or media formats, and it is the Developers responsibility to modernize them. The Designer/Developer will also learn about the museum's back-end systems, and should be able to respond accordingly if any problems arise. He or she may occasionally do some work on the back-end.
