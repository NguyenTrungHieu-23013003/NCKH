Under general supervision of the ECM Supervisor, the ECM Analyst is responsible for basic administration, process intake and support of the UCSF ECM system (Hyland’s Perceptive Content Server, aka ImageNow). This broadly includes clinical and administrative document imaging, storage, and access via desktop, web, and mobile clients, and via Citrix APeX desktop environment. The ECM Analyst will be responsible for supporting daily user imaging business operations; serving as a tier two to three Service Desk resource; working with business owners and functional administrators to analyze processes and/or workflow designs to develop imaging plans and business process models; developing and maintaining capture profiles and user replication policies; documenting, diagnosing, and resolving operational problems; maintaining security policies; performing basic ECM training; maintaining scripts, such as iScripts, eforms, and JavaScript. The ECM Analyst participates in documenting process and workflow requirements, and then works with the ECM Administrators to build solutions which fulfill these requirements. INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY UCSF is technologically and organizationally complex, with both centralized and departmental Information Technology (IT) units providing support for enterprise and specialized services. UCSF IT provides infrastructure, network, security, analytics, integration, and application support services, and is essential to the organization’s ability to support growth, mitigate security risk, and manage, store and share sensitive information. ABOUT UCSF The University of California, San Francisco (UCSF) is a leading university dedicated to promoting health worldwide through advanced biomedical research, graduate-level education in the life sciences and health professions, and excellence in patient care. It is the only campus in the 10-campus UC system dedicated exclusively to the health sciences.
