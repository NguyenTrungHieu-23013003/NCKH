Job Summary The IT Systems Administrator is responsible for administering, optimizing and supporting company-wide IT systems of a multi-site manufacturing organization. A certain degree of creativity and latitude is required. Relies on experience and judgment to plan and accomplish goals and projects. In addition, this position is also responsible for the Administration & management of all company Servers & related infrastructure, Active Directory, DNS, Office 365/Exchange environment, VMWare, NetApp, Backup and Disaster recovery. Stays current with technological developments and recommends ways to take advantage of new technologies. Must be familiar with ITIL Change and Problem Management Processes, Project Management as well as work well within a collaborative environment. Job Responsibilities Administers Windows servers at all locations (including Active Directory in a large environment, DNS, file/print servers, storage, domain controllers, production applications, Office 365, Email environment, builds, high availability, Disaster Recovery, batch scheduling, security administration, patch management, alert notifications, user administration, operational support, and backup/recovery) Install new / rebuild existing servers and configure hardware, peripherals, services, settings, directories, storage, etc. in accordance with standards and project/operational requirements Perform daily system monitoring, verifying the integrity and availability of all hardware, server resources, systems and key processes, reviewing system and application logs, and verifying completion of scheduled jobs such as backups Provide Tier III/IV support per request from various constituencies. Investigates and troubleshoots issues. Repairs and recovers from hardware or software failures Coordinates and communicates with impacted constituencies Coordinates, collaborates and communicates with IT 3rd party service providers Perform ongoing performance tuning, hardware upgrades, and resource optimization as required. Configure CPU, memory, and disk partitions as required Plan and undertake regular maintenance work across all IT systems Participates in the evaluation and recommendation of new technologies Must be on-call and available on a 24X7 Provide tier II/III support Other duties as assigned Skills/work Experience Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science, or a related field; or the equivalent in education/certification and work experience Requires minimum of 4 years’ experience in System Administration role Experience in the administration and technical support of LAN, replication software, and virtualization software such as VMware / Citrix Experience in SAP administration and Security Experienced in Windows OS Systems administration, with DNS, WINS, file access methods, system utilities, and Active Directory in a large environment. To include backup and recovery utilities Thorough ‘hands-on’ understanding of SAN/NAS management and configuration (NetApp preferred) Thorough understanding of all Microsoft Office products. Comprehensive knowledge to support SQL platforms, to include ability to write simple queries, understanding of joins and sub-queries, ability to diagnose SQL server issues from operations stand-point. Understanding of log-shipping, replication and database backup strategy concepts Comprehensive knowledge of the technical systems procurement and implementation life cycles Comprehensive knowledge of hardware, telecommunications, network, and personal computer analysis Requires multi-tasking and organizational skills, as well as stress management skills Project management skills a plus Good knowledge of IT Governance processes supporting problem management and change management a plus Excellent written and oral communication skills to effectively interact with all levels of the organization (including IT professionals)
