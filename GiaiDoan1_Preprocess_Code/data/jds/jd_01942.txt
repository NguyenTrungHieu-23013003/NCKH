Visit our website at http://www.azdhs.gov Infectious Disease Surveillance Epidemiologist Office of Infectious Disease Services Open until filled JOB SUMMARY This position serves as a journey-level epidemiologist responsible for carrying out a range of investigative and analytical epidemiologic activities related to the surveillance, detection, and prevention of diseases and injuries. The Epidemiologist works as a team or project member, under the direction of a senior epidemiologist or epidemiology program manager, conducting epidemiologic functions comprising surveillance, data collection, data analysis using epidemiologic methods, and assistance with epidemiologic investigations. This role conducts epidemiologic investigations used in preventing and controlling specific communicable and chronic diseases, Injuries, and other health conditions and applies findings to direct public health programs and recommend interventions and/or control measures. The individual in this role will develop and provide scientific and clinical advice and technical assistance to various public, private, and nonprofit health and health-related state and local agencies and organizations. All Arizona State employees operate within the Arizona Management System (AMS), an intentional, results-driven approach for doing the work of state government whereby every employee reflects on performance, reduces waste, and commits to continuous improvement with sustainable progress. Through AMS, every state employee seeks to understand customer needs, identify problems, improve processes, and measure results. State employees are highly engaged, collaborative and embrace a culture of public service. Responsibilities include: – Participating in conducting outbreak and other epidemiologic investigations by assisting with onsite review of facilities and procedures – Interviewing patients, health care providers, and others Involved – Assisting in collecting data from local agencies or other health entities – Participating in developing epidemiologic investigations – Using databases for analysis of health problems – Using surveillance systems to collect disease or health outcome data – Developing and providing scientific and clinical advice and technical assistance to various public, private, and nonprofit health and health-related state and local agencies and organizations – Serving as a scientific consultant for a public health program or project – Conducting literature reviews and assists in preparing reports from analyses of surveillance data, literature reviews, and other information using behavioral science, demographic and epidemiologic analytic techniques, and evaluation – Participating in after-hours activities including partner meetings, investigation, outbreak and emergency response as needed – Participating in writing grants and other funding proposals Requirements include: Comprehensive knowledge of contemporary principles and practices of epidemiology; research methods, procedures and techniques used identifying and evaluating disease characteristics; contemporary medical methods and practices used in the control of a variety of diseases; principles and practices applicable to the surveillance and investigation of diseases; public health surveillance Computerized database management, data analysis, program evaluation, literature search, effective verbal and written communication, and interpersonal skills Skills to conduct scientific studies required to identify and isolate various diseases; the compilation and the evaluation of technical epidemiological data; conducting investigations; interpersonal relations, as applied to contacts with representatives of other governmental agencies and the public Critical thinking about infectious disease causes, about approaches to analyzing, margining and interpreting data about how best to communicate complex information to the public and providers Support a diverse multi-cultural workforce that reflects the community, promotes equal opportunity at all levels of public employment, and creates an inclusive work environment that enables all individuals to perform to their fullest potential free from discrimination Preferred Qualifications: Masters of public health in Epidemiology and one year of professional experience in applied public health epidemiology Bachelors in public health or a science-related field and three years of experience in applied public health epidemiology Ideal candidate will have experience using statistical software, working with the different levels of US health care system and public health system, and working with electronic public health surveillance databases.
