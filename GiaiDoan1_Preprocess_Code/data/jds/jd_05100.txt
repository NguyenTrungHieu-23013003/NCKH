ROLE SUMMARY Responsible for developing, deploying and maintaining high performing and scalable quality process and systems in a fast-paced manufacturing environment through interacting and collaborating with plant and business functional managers. DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES Provide leadership and guidance for effective, timely communication, and appropriate visibility of quality issues to all levels of the organization to include: Final Inspection, In-Process Controls, Supplier Quality Assurance, Systems Auditing, Product Testing, Finished Goods Audit Provide leadership in implementation of quality systems and procedures. Contribute to regular quality cost data collection and maintains optimum balance between appraisal, prevention and failure control techniques Communicate Company quality objectives, policies, plans job responsibilities, authority and accountability to all departments Measure and report quality performance including conformity to engineering, quality standards, regulations, process capabilities and quality costs Support corrective/preventative action initiatives and short term containment of issues Provide and conduct audit systems related to process, procedures, and products. Document Company quality policies and procedures and establish Company quality goals in support of Company business plans Develop organizational structure for the performance of all phases of quality control Provide Quality department leadership; developing team by instilling a continuous improvement mindset and lead Quality Management Reviews Establish and implement quality specifications, testing procedures, and standards for raw materials and finished products Develop and recommend inspection and sampling techniques and quality control plans Conduct research on product defects and recommend modifications to products or quality standards where warranted Provide technical assistance to the purchasing department in selecting new vendors, purchase of raw materials, supplies and equipment Review complaints or warranty claims received to determine where quality control emphasis should be placed at the plant to eliminate problems. Establish and manage departmental budget and assist in the development of long range goals and objectives SKILLS & KNOWLEDGE Bachelor’s degree in a related technical field, preferably engineering Minimum 5+ years of manufacturing/quality control experience Min 5+ years of supervisory/management experience Advanced knowledge of quality policies and procedures Strong problem solving and interpersonal skills Excellent leadership skills for coaching technically based employees Exceptional written and verbal communication skills
