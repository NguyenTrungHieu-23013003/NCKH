We are a team of experimental and computational biologists with the goal of understanding how genetic differences between individuals create variability in cellular function and ultimately disease susceptibility. This effort builds on the development and application of cutting edge genomic (RNA-seq, ATAC-seq, single cell RNA-seq) and computational technologies (linear mixed models, deep learning). We focus on the human immune response as our model system and collaborate extensively with clinicians and immunologists. The research will increase our understanding of how genetics influence cellular function in health and disease. The Staff Research Associate I/II (SRA I/II) will play an important role in supporting various experimental efforts in the lab. The SRA I/II will interact primarily with the lab manager and graduate students.
