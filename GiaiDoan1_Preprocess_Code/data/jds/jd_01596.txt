Steer is seeking an entry-level analyst to join our Boston or New York offices. This is an exciting opportunity to be part of a team that works on a wide variety of high-profile transportation projects, including traffic and revenue studies for toll roads and managed lanes, ridership forecasting for high-speed rail, transportation economics and finance studies, and strategic planning. Candidates for this position should be highly motivated, with a strong capability for critical thinking, a broad range of quantitative research and analysis skills, considerable proficiency using computers, and a passion for all types of transportation. Desirable background and technical skills include: Bachelor of Science in engineering, economics, operations research or other quantitative field Internet and library research and data gathering Data analysis (including basic statistics and econometrics) Modeling (including travel demand modeling and simulation) Geographic information systems (GIS) Successful entry-level analysts at Steer are able to learn quickly; to apply their knowledge to solve problems involving new situations; to manage their time and prioritize tasks; and to communicate well with peers, senior staff and clients. They work closely with more senior personnel on small teams, and are expected to perform challenging work and quickly assume increasing levels of responsibility.
