Organization & Position Summary: The Arc Minnesota promotes and protects the human rights of people with intellectual and developmental disabilities, actively supporting them and their families in a lifetime of full inclusion and participation in their communities throughout the state of Minnesota. The Arc pursues lasting community change and connections that support people with intellectual and developmental disabilities and their families in creating a good life. This position will grow a regional quality council with county lead agencies and provider organizations. Recruit and on-board parents, self-advocates, providers for the council; develop a quality monitoring system that includes review team members, training, and communications; works closely with quality council members and review teams to report findings and make recommendations to State Quality Council to improve quality of services. Demonstrates and models The Arc Minnesota’ Values: “We value each person”, “We are connectors”, “We are courageous”, and “We are accountable” on a daily basis. Major Responsibilities: Leads the Regional Quality Council (RQC) through key planning and collaboration Participate on other related committees and utilize knowledge in the work of the RQC Develop and implement strategies/materials to educate and inform, parents, persons with disabilities, professionals about the RQC. Develop and maintain website for the State and Regional Quality Councils. Conducts interviews with project participants using quality review materials/tools Review and update processes for the person-centered quality reviews Record and maintain interview information in database from person-centered quality reviews Trains volunteers and interns on person-centered quality review processes Identifies best practices that promote a higher quality of life for persons with disabilities Works with the State Quality Council to share insights and to assist in the implementation of the person centered quality review. Analyzes interview data to discern trends in the quality of services Qualifications: BA/BS in human services or related field required 3-5 years’ human services experience required Excellent project management, planning, and facilitation skills Excellent leadership skills Compensation: Competitive Benefits and Salary
