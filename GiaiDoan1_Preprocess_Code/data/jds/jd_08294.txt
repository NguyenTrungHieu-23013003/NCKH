Under general direction of the Water Resources Division Manager, plans, organizes, directs, and supervises one or more sections in the Water Resources Division; evaluates, develops, recommends, monitors and updates technical programs for the Division, such as the City's potable and recycled water systems, wastewater conveyance, treatment and disposal systems, and the storm water management system; represents the Water Resources Division when working with outside water, wastewater and recycled water purveyors and wholesalers, Federal, State and local regulatory agencies, City departments/divisions, and other agencies; serves as the custodian of the Division's hydraulic models; performs the duties of the Division Manager during his/her absence; and performs other duties as assigned. DISTINGUISHING CHARACTERISTICS This is a single position, management level classification in the Water Resources Division that manages one or more sections within the Water Resources Division and is responsible for the Division's technical programs. Work is performed with considerable latitude in the application of policy, within general guidelines and professional and administrative standards. This classification is distinguished from Water Resources Division Manager in that the latter has overall responsibility for the entire division, including the division's leadership and administrative responsibilities. It is further distinguished from the Public Works Supervisor which is a managerial classification with responsibility for a single section in the Division. SUPERVISION RECEIVED General direction is provided by the Water Resources Division Manager. SUPERVISION EXERCISED Provides supervision to assigned managerial, professional, technical, clerical, and temporary employees. Examples of Important and Essential Functions: Program Management Functions Provides program management in the areas of planning, hydraulic modeling, installation, repair, maintenance and operation of utility systems; oversees potable water, recycled water, wastewater treatment plant and sewer master plans, the urban water management plan, connection fee studies, and asset management program; provides project management for the Division's non-CIP projects; acts as Division's representative and liaison with outside agencies and other City divisions/departments, including Planning and Engineering; collaborates with Engineering Division assisting with preparation of Capital Improvement Plan projects for Water Resources Division assets; reviews construction plans and specifications; provides oversight for the Water Resources Division's contracted projects, services and purchases; may inspect work sites to ensure progress and compliance with City standards and contract terms and conditions; provides oversight of and monitors compliance with environmental regulations from Federal, State and local agencies, such as the California Environmental Protection Agency (Cal-EPA), State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB), Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), Bay Area Air Quality Management District (BAAQMD), and other agencies; attends meetings, conducts research, compiles information, prepares reports, assists in policy development, completes special projects, and makes presentations. Supervisory Functions Recommends for hire, trains, evaluates, and mentors assigned employees; monitors timecards and all employee leave balances; evaluates operations and activities of sections; develops benchmark standards for performance measures and promotes continuous improvement; and ensures safety training of all staff and that the work environment is in compliance with safety standards. Administrative Functions Assists in the development, implementation and monitoring of the Division's goals, objectives, policies and procedures; monitors work activities, projects and programs; reviews and evaluates work products, methods and procedures; assembles information and projections to aid in the preparation of annual budget requests, fee studies and rate analyses; monitors established budget, and authorized budgetary and purchasing procedures; coordinates Division activities with other divisions, department and outside agencies; prepares clear and concise written reports; makes presentations to the City Council, city management, boards and commissions, government agencies, and community- based organizations; responds to inquiries and requests for information; and participates on division, department and City-wide committees. Job Related and Essential Qualifications: Demonstrated Knowledge of: Principles and practices of supervision, training, mentoring, and performance management; management principles and practices, including planning and organizing work, establishing goals and objectives, budgeting, and cost control; principles, practices, and techniques for program development, administration and evaluation; general principles and practices of water/wastewater engineering as applied to design, construction, operation, and maintenance of potable and recycled water distribution systems, wastewater conveyance, treatment and disposal systems, hydrology, hydraulics and engineering economics; wastewater treatment plant operations and maintenance; materials, procedures and equipment used in construction, operations and maintenance of potable and recycled water distribution, wastewater conveyance, treatment and disposal facilities; construction and project management principles and practices related to implementation of improvement projects in a municipal setting; principles of asset management; proper conduct of engineering studies; local, regional, state and federal laws and regulations related to the conveyance, treatment and disposal of wastewater, distribution of potable and recycled water, and storm water management; generally accepted water, wastewater and storm water principles; computerized software systems used in day-to-day operations including CAD, hydraulic modeling applications, computerized maintenance management systems, geographical information systems, and supervisory control and data acquisition; principles of budget development, administration, and expenditure control systems; principles, practices, rules, and codes relating to work safety management; and pertinent local, State and Federal laws, ordinances and rules. Demonstrated Skills to: Establish effective working relationships with a wide variety of City staff, project contractors and representatives of other governmental agencies, professional contacts, City officials and the public by demonstrating good judgment, initiative and resourcefulness; assemble and analyze data, preparing comprehensive and accurate reports, formulating policy and service recommendations; critically assess situations, solve problems, work effectively under pressure, within deadlines; read and interpret design plans; effectively manage consultants and contractors; effectively communicate policies, procedures, City codes and operations to other City staff and the public; evaluate and recommend changes to operations as needed; analyze and solve complex problems involving multi-department or multi-agency collaboration; effectively coordinate with other agencies on collaborative projects; maintain accurate records; effectively communicate both orally and in writing; supervise and evaluate staff; prepare, administer and monitor budgets; work independently under general direction, exercising initiative and creativity in performing assigned duties; work well under pressure of deadlines; be politically astute but apolitical; and exemplify an enthusiastic, resourceful, and effective customer service attitude with all who are contacted in the course of the work. Ability to: Effectively apply the required knowledge and skills in the daily performance of assigned duties; learn, interpret, and apply City, department and division rules, regulations, policies and practices that are necessary for this job classification. Experience, Education, and Training Guidelines: Any combination of experience, education, and training that would provide the best-qualified candidates. A typical way to obtain the knowledge, skills, and abilities would be: Experience: Five years of progressively responsible experience in water/wastewater/storm water systems planning and design, project management, or a closely related field. Public sector experience with a water or wastewater utility, engineering firm, construction firm, environmental related industry, or similar organization is highly desirable. Education: Equivalent to a Bachelor's Degree from an accredited college or university with major coursework in civil, chemical, mechanical, sanitary, or environmental engineering, physical science, project management, environmental science, or a related field. Training: Any recent training such as academic courses and certification programs that are relevant to this job classification. License: Possession of a valid California Drivers' License and a satisfactory driving record, as determined by the City of Livermore. Certification: A certificate of registration as a Professional Civil Engineer is desirable. Other Requirements: Willingness and ability to work the hours necessary to accomplish the job requirements; be on-call in the event of an emergency; travel and attend conferences, meetings, seminars and courses during work and non-work hours; work under adverse conditions, such as around heavy construction equipment and in inclement weather. Special Requirements: Essential duties require the mental and/or physical ability to: work in a standard office environment; drive a vehicle; read fine print; converse over the telephone, two-way radio, in person, or around the noise of heavy construction equipment; move across rough terrain and bridge scaffolding; bend, stoop, and reach; climb into and out of sewers and trenches, up 25-foot ladders and several flights of stairs; use drafting instruments, calculators and personal computers; safely lift and maneuver office supplies, blueprints, and boxes weighing up to 30 pounds. Additional Information: The City of Livermore is an equal opportunity employer and supports workforce diversity.
