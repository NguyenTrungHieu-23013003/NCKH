Data Compliance Analyst ( Job Number: 180001XD ) Description The Chicago Public Schools (CPS) has set ambitious goals to ensure that every child—in every school and every neighborhood—has access to a world-class learning experience from birth, resulting in graduation from high school college- and career-ready. Information Technology Services is seeking an ISBE (Illinois Board of Education) Data Compliance Analyst to serve as the primary point of contact to perform data quality assurance analysis and reject clean-up (between the CPS and ISBE systems), take the lead to resolve duplicate student records/IDs in the CPS student information system, and interface with ISBE personnel and various CPS and ITS teams. This position requires strong interpersonal, communication, organizational, and analytical skills. This position must also have the ability to work effectively with ITS teams, Central Office staff, internal and external School Administrators, and CPS’ ISBE contacts. Responsibilities: Take the lead to resolve duplicate student records created in the CPS student information system: Perform investigate analysis in one or more CPS student information systems and in the ISBE system, in order to determine which student record/ID is the correct one to keep, and be responsible (through to close) of all tickets pertaining to Duplicate ID requests Give top priority in working directly with school users to provide guidance and inform them of which Student ID is the correct one to use Determine whether manual clean-up is needed before the duplicate records can be merged and reach out to the applicable internal teams who may need to take action in their respective systems or in the ISBE student information system. Utilize the Duplicate Student Resolution Tool to merge records, to include resolving any errors that may result during the merge process and working closely with the Student Information System Technical teams who support the tool. Work closely with various Central Office teams to prioritize and determine the order in which duplicate student records may need to be resolved Manage CPS’ ISBE mailbox Resolve ISBE compliance errors, such as missing data (i.e. grade, homeless, english learner, RSSP, etc.) or address corrections in the CPS student information system by reaching out to schools (emails or phone calls), as needed, and/or working with applicable ITS and/or CPS teams. Perform manual edits in the ISBE student information system (enrollment entries and exits; state ID creation, and other data quality cleanup) Work with CPS’ Office of Teaching & Learning to ensure course code mapping is correct Interface with other school districts across the State of Illinois (emails and phone calls) Maintain a cohesive relationship with CPS’ ISBE contacts Inform ITS Data Solutions/Data Analytics team of the duplicate and shared state ID resolutions to be implemented in the nightly load files Qualifications: At a minimum, Associates Degree or equivalent technical or business certifications and experience required 2 - 4 years of experience in a fast paced, highly interactive customer service related field Prior experience supporting end users in an education environment desired Must have excellent customer service and interpersonal skills Excellent verbal and written communication skills Strong analytical skills and attention to detail required Deep understanding of CPS’ Student Information Systems desired Problem solver with demonstrated experience troubleshooting issues and following through to resolution Strong organizational skills Strength of character to work in a fast-paced environment while juggling competing priorities and maintaining deadlines and goals Ability to work well both independently and as part of a team Diligent in following processes and procedures Ability to comfortably interface with all levels of management and staff Residency Requirement: As a condition of employment with the Chicago Public Schools (CPS), employees are required to live within the geographic boundaries of the City of Chicago within six months of his or her CPS hire date and maintain residency throughout their employment with the district.
