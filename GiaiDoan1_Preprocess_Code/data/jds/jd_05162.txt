— Compliance Manager for Quality Assurance SUMMARY OF RESPONSIBILITIES: The Compliance Manager for Quality Assurance is responsible for managing and leading the Quality Assurance team. The Compliance Manager for Quality Assurance will report directly to the Director of Training and Development and work with the Compliance Officer in daily duties and responsibilities. Manage and lead the Quality Assurance team across the state of Texas Oversee the Quality Assurance team and provide guidance in developing action plans for corrective actions at a site level Review and develop action plans at a management level Audit comprehensive reports regarding site visit findings and identify patterns for areas of improvement Monitor regional quality/compliance performance and report findings to Compliance Officer and direct supervisor Responsible for maintenance of quality assurance data and trackers Conduct periodic internal reviews to ensure quality assurance deadlines are followed Audits all quality assurance forms Communicate and work with cross-functional teams to solve non-compliance issues Communicate with districts and schools in progress of fulfilling compliance requirements Serve as sponsor for quality improvement action plans at the management level Educate and instruct management and the Quality Assurance team on quality, compliance and workflow trainings Oversee NDL checklist for all sites to stay in compliance Responsible for submitting proof of training to the Human Resources department Understanding of CACFP/SFSP TDA and USDA handbooks and stay abreast of appropriate updates Assist the Compliance Officer as needed Responsible for completing time and effort reports to account for work activity and payroll confirmation Travel to all Regional Offices on a quarterly basis Additional duties as assigned. QUALIFICATIONS: Possess a bachelor’s degree in law, finance, business administration, or related field. Master’s degree preferred. Job related experience may be substituted in lieu of education. Minimum of five (5) years’ experience in quality control, quality assurance, and/or compliance experience preferred 5+ years’ supervisory experience Demonstrate critical reasoning skills and conflict resolution skills Ability to pay attention to detail, execute assigned tasks and work with a high degree of accuracy. Excellent leadership, interpersonal, communication, team building and influencing skills Strong organizational, analytical, problem solving, and decision-making skills Ability to clearly and effectively present information in both written and verbal formats to a varying audience.
