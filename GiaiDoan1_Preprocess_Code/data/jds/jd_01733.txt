The Department encourages people of all backgrounds to apply, including people of color, immigrants, refugees, women, LGBTQ, people with disabilities, veterans, and those with lived experiences._ The Department of Community and Human Services (DCHS) provides equitable opportunities for people to be healthy, happy, self-reliant and connected to community. King County Behavioral Health and Recovery Division (BHRD) and its community partners are in the process of becoming a fully integrated managed care system, integrating behavioral health and medical care clinically and with payment processes with five managed healthcare organizations. This position will work with the division executive leadership team and lead the design and implementation of incentive structures for value/outcome-based payment and development of population health management strategies for across King County's public behavioral healthcare system. Concurrently, King County's Accountable Community of Health (ACH) is leading countywide implementation of bi-directional medical-behavioral health care practice transformation and value-based payment methods to implement Washington State's five-year Medicaid Transformation Project. This position will support and provide leadership to ensure alignment, financial viability, and successful implementation during the transition to fully integrated managed care. The work has high visibility to King County leadership, managed care organizations, the ACH, and the state Health Care Authority. This position will work as part of the BHRD management team. The position will coordinate responsibilities with other Division and Departmental managers and the Performance Measurement and Evaluation team, depending upon the specific activities. _WHO MAY APPLY:_ This is a career service position open to all qualified applicants. The Department of Community and Human Services values diverse perspectives and life experiences.
