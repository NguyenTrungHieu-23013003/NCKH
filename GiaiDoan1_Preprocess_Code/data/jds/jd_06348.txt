Overview Intuit is a leading provider of business and financial management solutions for small and mid-sized businesses, consumers, financial institutions and accounting professionals. You probably know us by our flagship products, QuickBooks, TurboTax and Mint, but that's just the start. We're taking on exciting challenges, such as SaaS and mobile apps. Over 50 million users and seven million small businesses depend on Intuit because we innovate at the crossroads of real customer problems and breakthrough technology. Intuit's Corporate Strategy & Development (CS&D;) team works on highly visible, direction-setting strategy and acquisition initiatives at the corporate level and across all of Intuit’s businesses. Come join the CS&D; team as an Integration Manager and play an instrumental role supporting due diligence, integration planning and execution phases of strategic acquisitions. Responsibilities Develop the right integration strategy and success metrics based on the business objectives of the deal Participate in due diligence and decision making as a member of the core deal team Manage the end-to-end integration process, from creating a holistic integration plan to achieving deal specific value and synergies, driving an issue-free Day One, and leading the ongoing integration project, by working closely with representatives from the business unit, functional groups, and acquired team Actively manage integration risks; identify and solve cross-functional integration issues Stay abreast of Intuit’s priorities and exemplify culture, to effectively support acquired company through the integration Continuously improve Intuit’s M &A; integration capability through process improvement, performance measurement, and team development Qualifications We expect individuals in CS &D; to exhibit a broad set of skills including a keen sense of strategic thinking and rigorous analytics, solid written and verbal communications ability, influencing skills, inspirational leadership, and understanding stakeholder needs. In addition, the Integration Manager should also be able to quickly establish rapport, credibility, and trust across internal stakeholders and with founders/ executives at target companies, and be viewed as a team player. BA/BS degree 3+ years of M&A; integration and project management experience Strong business acumen and understanding of go to market operations Ability to work independently as well as under the guidance of a senior integration leader Ability to work well under deadlines and manage multiple priorities Highly organized, detail-oriented, and proficient at MS Office and Google Suite Willingness to travel (as needed)
