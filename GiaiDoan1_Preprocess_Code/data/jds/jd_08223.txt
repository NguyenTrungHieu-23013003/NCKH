We pride ourselves on our people-first culture and a low-ego workplace that embraces experimentation, collaboration and continuous improvement. We have a fun office environment located in Davis Square (Somerville, MA) and offer competitive pay and excellent benefits. Technical Producers in the Production Support department are responsible for developing and reviewing technical specifications for website work and ensuring that day-to-day operations run smoothly. Technical Producers act as a liaison for clients’ web marketing teams and Velir’s technical delivery team. Technical Producers actively participate in the entire support development lifecycle over extended periods. Technical Producers work closely with internal team members to identify challenges and recommend solutions. Technical Producers are knowledgeable about web and content management systems standards and best practices and work to apply these standards to solve a client's business needs. Responsibilities Partner with account team to create, maintain and promote successful client relationships Manage all development phases of ongoing support engagements from requirements gathering through deployment Gather business and functional requirements with client stakeholders Document requirements and requested changes Facilitate approval of requirements Present solutions and recommendations based on client objectives Participate in creative brainstorming sessions and generate ideas for client campaigns and web sites Write clear and concise test cases for QA Validate development work against requirements and lead client demonstrations Lead project budgeting, planning, scheduling, contract adherence, risk management, and execution within budget and schedule Skills & Qualifications Knowledge Skills and Abilities: Strong communication skills and ability to work well in teams Ability to manage multiple, ongoing project timelines and/or support Program Managers with larger projects/budgets Must have moderate understanding of web development technologies (CMS, .NET, HTML/CSS/JS) but not necessarily the ability to program Working knowledge of scoping & estimate creation Demonstrated ability to lead project teams and manage project schedules and budgets Knowledge of web design and development lifecycles Familiarity with user experience concepts and web development trends Experience and Education: Minimum 2 years of experience managing web projects Experience using development management systems, ideally JIRA and Atlassian toolset Experience working with content management systems from a delivery and editing standpoint Physical Requirements: Frequent sitting at a desk performing work on a computer.
