Position Title: Logistics Associate Director Reporting to: Functionally accountable to the Director of Clinical Operations Location: Boston (Up to 30% to sites) Position O verview: The Logistics Associate Director provides overall direction within the logistics team in relation to international and local logistics; technical support to field counterparts; develops logistics and site operations tools, and leads biomedical equipment and fleet management The responsible plays a critical role in PIH’s global operations by ensuring products are delivered to the country sites and ultimately the point of care. This position will be responsible for moving product both domestically and internationally. In the short term, this role will be focused on day to day logistics and the development of processes and procedures to ensure inventory accuracy and proper tracking of product. Longer term, this position will focus on working with country sites to optimize material handling, inventory systems and general logistics process improvement initiatives such as fleet and biomedical equipment management. Responsibilities : Local and international logistics: (40%) Lead the local and International coordination of logistics processes including: warehousing, coordination with vendors, shipments and custom procedures Provide technical guidance on local inventory management, address issues/discrepancies, standardize & manage cycle count and yearly inventory procedures Design, implement, oversee special projects, stock movement for new wards/services, vaccination campaigns) Logistics technical field support: (25%) Counterpart for site logistics & operations teams acting as a technical resource, providing guidance for daily operations, identifying challenges and gaps, and advising on long term strategic goals Conduct regular check-ins with site counterparts for addressing issues, monitoring progress, identifying operational risks and opportunities, sharing updates Develop/adapt and implement logistics and site operations tools and guidelines including: existing manuals & SOPS, data, standardize format across countries. Other: (35%) Lead the implementation of a biomed strategy across PIH sites, define needs, and guide sites on medical equipment management (budget, inventory, maintenance, contracts) Develop tools for equipment inventory and fleet management (paper, Excel, OpenBoxes) and share with sites, guiding implementation and monitoring their usage Standardize and disseminate vehicle safety protocols – equipment, training, tracking, SOPs Develop protocols for equipment maintenance & repair. Guide implementation and monitor their usage Oversee facilities support functions from the Boston office for PIH sites globally. As a primary point of contact for facilities initiatives, develop systems and communications processes to shepherd facilities work taking place at PIH sites Qualifications: Master’s degree or equivalent work experience required, Concentration in Business, Engineering, Supply Chain, Public Health, or International Development preferred 5 or more years of experience in a supply chain, operations, public health or healthcare setting required. Software skills including MS Office (Access & Excel focus) required Prior international experience in a developing country required Clear, concise communication skills, verbally and in writing Calm and flexible disposition with ability to operate in a resource limited environment Ability to prioritize work and meet deadlines among competing priorities French and/or Haitian Creole language skills required Exemplary interpersonal skills; ability to collaborate effectively with culturally diverse staff across departments and country. Interest in social justice strongly desirable Organizational Profile Partners In Health (PIH) is an international health organization relentlessly committed to improving the health of the poor and marginalized. PIH partners with local governments to build local capacity and works closely with impoverished communities to deliver high-quality health care, address the root causes of illness, train providers, advance research, and advocate for global policy change. PIH currently has implementation programs in Haiti, Rwanda, Lesotho, Liberia, Malawi, Peru, Mexico, Russia, Sierra Leone and Navajo Nation. Through our partnership with Harvard Medical School and the Brigham and Women’s Hospital, we support a wide range of programs in dozens of other countries around the world.
