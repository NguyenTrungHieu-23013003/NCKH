Position Description Ally Financial Inc.'s (Ally) Corporate Insurance Department (CID) is responsible for implementation of Ally's enterprise risk financing strategy, primarily including structure and placement of corporate insurance programs. By managing insurance relationships on an enterprise basis and using scale to improve cost and terms, Ally optimizes coverage quality for its insurance purchases. Job Responsibilities Corporate Insurance seeks to maintain a world-class insurance risk management function that creates value for Ally by: Protecting Ally, its employees, and its assets by transferring risk to 3rd party insurers Implementing innovative and economical insurance solutions tailored to Ally's risk profile Achieving the most efficient long-term corporate cost of insurable risk Effectively fulfilling applicable regulatory and contractual requirements The Enterprise Risk Financing Analyst will support these goals through the following primary responsibilities: Compile underwriting data submissions and support marketing and renewal of insurance programs, including direction of brokerage strategy, negotiation of terms and conditions, etc. Direct annual insurance policy reviews to identify material coverage changes and ensure policies align with expected renewal product Prepare and review departmental reports and presentations for CID and Ally Risk leadership Monitor CID's counterparty requirements, including counterparty limits and OFAC compliance processes Manage CID's accounting and budgetary planning processes, including monthly reconciliations and forecasting as necessary Develop and manage internal and external relationships with business partners, brokers, and insurers Communicate insurance coverage to business units and functions through circulation of policy summaries, educational presentations, and other support where necessary Serve as internal consultant and insurance subject matter expert for internal teams from procurement, transportation finance, etc. Support due diligence processes and manage transition and integration of risk management for new acquisitions or divestitures Qualifications Education Undergraduate degree in risk management, accounting, finance, economics or related area Insurance designation (e.g., CPCU, ARM, etc.) preferred Skill Requirements Project management skills and ability to effectively prioritize tasks Ability to read and interpret technical legal and insurance documents Ability to effectively communicate ideas and issues with executive management.
