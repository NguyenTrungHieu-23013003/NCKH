_ARIZONA DEPARTMENT OF VETERANS’ SERVICES_ _For Arizona Veterans and those who care for them._ _JOIN OUR TEAM AND SERVE THOSE WHO HAVE SERVED YOU!_ PUBLIC INFORMATION OFFICER III LEGISLATIVE LIAISON Director’s Office- Phoenix 3839 North 3rd Street, Suite 209 Phoenix, Arizona 85012 https://dvs.az.gov/ JOB SUMMARY: Under the direction of the Deputy Director, this position is responsible for monitoring, researching, analyzing and testifying on various pieces of veteran-related legislation. Works with legislators, their staff, lobbyist and constitutes in the handling of legislative matters; works with agency leadership to coordinate the agency’s positions and testimony on assigned bills, ensuring that legal, fiscal and administrative implications are analyzed and communicated in a timely and appropriate manner; keeps track of federal legislation that may impact the agency; serves as the agency contact for constituent inquiries from other governmental agencies; acts as Department Ombudsman and contact of for the Arizona State Ombudsman; responds to a variety of inquires from constituents and other governmental entities; serves as technical resource and back-up to the PIO; drives on state business; and performs other job-related duties as assigned KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES (KSAs): Knowledge of: The agency’s mission, objectives, organization, programs, policies and procedures Agency legislation and goals The veteran community The legislative process, including bill introduction and procedure, testimony, protocol and committee structure Fundamental research and statistical procedures Methods of obtaining data and basic procedures in the analysis and interpretation of this information Skills in: Oral and written communication. Math sufficient to compile information and provide statistics. Establishing and maintaining interpersonal relationships. Using a PC and associated software (e.g., Microsoft Office Suite. Analytical and problem solving. Preparing materials for oral, visual and written presentation. Organization and time management. Record maintenance. Ability to: Speak and write clearly, concisely and effectively Read, interpret and apply laws, rules, regulations, policies and /or procedures Review and/or edit documents for accuracy and completeness. Use of logic to analyze or identify underlying principles, reasons or facts associated with information or data to draw conclusions Take minutes, compose correspondence and prepare reports Drive on state business SPECIAL SELECTION FACTOR(S): This position requires possession of and ability to retain a current, valid state-issued driver’s license appropriate to the assignment. Employees who drive on state business are subject to driver’s license record checks, must maintain acceptable driving records and must complete any required driver training (see Arizona Administrative Code R2-10-207.12) SELECTIVE PREFERENCE(S): A bachelor’s degree in public affairs, public policy, public management or related field; and two years of experience working within the legislative process BENEFITS: We offer a comprehensive benefits package that includes: Accrued vacation and sick days; 10 paid holidays per year Affordable medical, dental, life insurance and short-term disability – visit http://www.benefitoptions.az.gov/ Top-ranking defined benefit retirement plan – visit https://www.azasrs.gov Deferred compensation 457 plan; Flexible Spending Accounts Health Savings Account; Discount Home and Auto Insurance Wellness program CONTACT INFORMATION: _Email_ ___[email protected]_ _or call (602) 542-4700 for assistance with the application process._ _Persons with a disability may request a reasonable accommodation such as a sign language interpreter or an alternative format by contacting the Employment Manager at (602) 234-8422.
