The Systems Administrator is responsible for implementing, maintaining, and upgrading system software applications and hardware in accordance with established procedures. Working collaboratively with the Project Director and Project Managers, as well as, other source owners, the Systems Administrator will develop a project plan to establish project timelines, tasks, and milestones in collaboration with the vendor, as well as serve as a liaison to internal and external hospital departments, outside clients, and vendors. Other responsibilities include, but are not limited to, participating in the workflow re-design in relation to system functionality, evaluating issues and making recommendations when appropriate, preparing and maintaining application test scripts, integrated test scripts, and regression test scripts, assisting in the development and evaluation of the effectiveness of the system downtime procedures, developing, monitoring, troubleshooting and supporting multiple system interfaces to include laboratory instruments, HL7 interfaces to external systems, multiple billing systems, and remote laboratories, system administration and support for ad hoc reporting, coordinate and assist the training staff in the development of end-user training materials and appropriate training materials and data, monitoring the progress of the implementations, provide end user support including off ours support, and report project status information as requested. Minimum Qualifications Bachelor's degree and 3 years relevant experience (preferably in a laboratory or clinical healthcare setting) or equivalent combination of education and experience. Must be self-directed with strong interpersonal, communication, analytic and computer skills. Experience supporting Laboratory Information Systems (LIS) or Medical Technologist experience in a laboratory preferred.
