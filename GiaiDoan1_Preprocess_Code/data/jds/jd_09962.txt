Technology Opportunity in Financial Services Information Security Architect Accepting candidates that will report to one of the three following locations: 20 E Thomas Rd. Suite 2000 Phoenix, AZ 85012 Summary: The Information Security Architect leads design and implementation of information security initiatives and operations for the Firm, working to identify, manage, and reduce information security risk. The successful Information Security Architect demonstrates strong knowledge of technology risk management, experience with the information security controls, communicates well, is trustworthy in confidential matters, maintains credibility with stakeholders, and his/her capabilities and energies to meet business objectives. Keys: IT security, IT architect, information technology Responsibilities: Proactively and collaboratively works with business departments to develop and implement policies and procedures that meet business goals for productivity, profitability, and security; Identifies, evaluates, and reports on information assets and information security threats and vulnerabilities, including yet not limited to: Application vulnerability assessments to identify and prioritize security exposures in applications; Security risk assessments on vendors and business partners to assess suitability related to information security; System and network changes for security risks. Designs, implements and maintains information security and technology tools and systems; Deploys and administers security software solutions as needed; Develops business-relevant metrics to measure the efficiency and effectiveness of the Firm’s information security management program, including forecasting appropriate resource allocations and maturity of the program; Coordinates and supports security audits and assessments; Advises Management on industry developments in business practice, technology, security issues and legislation that impact the Firm’s information security posture; Performs security incident response with investigation, resolution and closure including lessons learned; Manages projects and implements initiatives surrounding data security and privacy; Coordinates the use of external resources involved in the information security management program, including, but not limited to, interviewing, assisting in negotiating contracts and fees and managing external resources; Travel is expected [under 25%] All other duties as assigned. Education Requirements: Bachelor’s degree in information technology, business administration or a related field preferred. Candidates with a minimum of a high school diploma (or equivalent) in combination with at least 7 years’ experience in an information technology role, inclusive of at least four years in information security will be considered. Basic Requirements: Proven ability to effectively analyze and develop options that balance business needs with information security threats; Skilled at building strong relationships, both internally with business and technology leaders and other leaders of the information security team, and externally with service providers and business partners; Advanced trouble-shooting and subject matter expertise with information security and technology tools; Strong familiarity with security issues surrounding web application security and experience in testing such applications for vulnerabilities; Ability to lead and motivate cross-functional, interdisciplinary teams to achieve tactical and strategic goals; Experience assessing third parties for infrastructure security controls and general security practices; Experience developing and implementing security policies and/or standards; Excellent working knowledge of operating systems and related applications; Strong understanding of networking protocols and networking concepts; Experience with deploying and securing Internet applications; Ability to research, analyze and resolve complex problems with minimal supervision and escalate issues as appropriate; Strong analytical skills; Excellent written and verbal communication skills; Ability to partner with and influence a variety of stakeholders to ensure security requirements are understood and met; Able to work independently and be a self-starter, managing multiple tasks according to priorities, and results oriented and proven ability to meet deadlines. Preferred Requirements: Professional certification such as CISSP
