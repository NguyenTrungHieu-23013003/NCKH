Salary Range $55,789-$72,526/annually, DOE Grade 117 Work Calendar 12 Months Job Summary The Business Systems Analyst will provide analysis, design, development, testing, and implementation of processes used in Information Technology. Business Systems Analyst will act as a liaison between functional user and technical systems groups; between internal and external stakeholders in the development or modification of typically complex information systems. Essential Functions 25% - Collects, analyzes and interprets business process information to identify the scope and impact of business problems; documents process workflow 20% - Facilitates implementation of business and/or technology solutions including acceptance testing processes 20% - Researches industry standards, trends, best practices and emerging technologies and makes recommendations for implementation 15% - Provides professional level staff support to committees, advisory groups and others that may include performing research, authoring documents and making presentations; facilitates team meetings 10% - Writes project documents that include defining strategy, resources and timeframes 10% - Performs other duties as assigned Minimum Qualifications Bachelor’s Degree in information technology or related field and two years of business systems analysis experience including business process design, business requirements definition, workflow documentation, project coordination and acceptance testing. OR An equivalent combination of education and experience sufficient to successfully perform the essential duties of the job such as those listed above, unless otherwise subject to any other requirements set forth in law or regulation Desired Qualifications At least 4 years of experience in a business process analysis role working directly with an Information Technology department Experience facilitating institution-wide IT systems or processes Knowledge or Skills Possessed: (higher Education Experience preferred) Principles and techniques of facilitating process analysis Methods and techniques of collecting, analyzing, and re-engineering business processes Diagnosing problems, determining business issues and developing and implementing solutions Compiling information or data and authoring reports and preparing presentations for internal and external stakeholders.
