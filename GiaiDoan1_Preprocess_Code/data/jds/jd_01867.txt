Job Summary : With supervision, the Actuarial Student performs varied actuarial functions for Group lines of business with an emphasis on extraction and summary of data to support development of analysis of mortality, morbidity, persistency, and expenses. Other functions include supervised assistance in pricing, product development, and aggregate rate adequacy analysis. Duties and Responsibilities : Develops summary of data to support quarterly loss experience analysis for distribution to Underwriting and Claims. Supports development of in depth loss experience analysis to drive continuous improvements to manual rate basis and rating/underwriting structure and practices. Gathers data to enable reporting of case level adjustments to manual rate basis. Develops summaries of persistency results by selected parameters. Gathers and summarizes data needed to support periodic expense reviews. Develops reports summarizing financial results by critical parameters including sales office, broker, and client. Develops and maintains actuarial databases required to perform desired analyses. Provides data summaries to support pricing and product development projects as required. Provides assistance with PACS manual rate testing. Provides ad hoc support to Sales and/or Underwriting as required. Continuous progress on actuarial exams. All other duties as assigned. Education, Qualifications and Experience : Undergraduate degree preferably in Actuarial Science, Math, Statistics, or other programs focused on quantitative analysis required. Completion of 1-3 Society of Actuaries exams. Skilled in spreadsheet and database software usage. Ability to consolidate large amounts of data to help identify key findings. Good verbal and written communication skills. Must be able to read and interpret complex charts and statistics from printed sources.
