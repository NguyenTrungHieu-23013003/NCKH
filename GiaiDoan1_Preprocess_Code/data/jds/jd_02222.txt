Applicants who are strongly being considered for employment must submit to a national criminal history background check. Performs advanced and/or managerial database administration work. Work involves coordinating the planning, development, maintenance, and monitoring of integrated database systems; evaluating and reviewing database policies and procedures; and maintaining data integrity. May supervise the work of others. Works under minimal supervision with considerable latitude for the use of initiative and independent judgment. Additional Military Crosswalk (occupational specialty code) information can be accessed at: http://www.hr.sao.state.tx.us/Compensation/JobDescriptions.aspx. Essential Job Functions: Oversees and performs the design, development, tuning and maintenance of databases including structures, behaviors, performance, security, and recovery. Ensures that data integrity and data security is maintained. Monitors database performance and implements efficiency improvements. Identifies and resolves database issues and problems. Provides technical assistance for development of complex SQL, functions, stored procedures and triggers. Performs data migration activities as needed. Plans for future data needs ensuring that needed capacity is available. Designs and implements procedures necessary to save and recover databases from hardware and software failures. Develops and maintains standards and procedures for security, backup/recovery, database usage and performance/tuning. Evaluates and recommends system and database management software and support tools. Provides support for other RDBMS systems as needed. Participates in the Disaster Recovery process as required. Maintains current knowledge and expertise in database technology. Perform on-call or scheduled after hours work as required. May supervise the work of others. Perform other related duties as assigned. Attend work regularly in accordance with agency leave and attendance policies. Comply with all applicable agency policies and procedures, including safety and standards of conduct. Minimum Qualifications: Education: Graduation from an accredited four year college or university with major course work in computer science, management information systems, or a related discipline. Experience: Five (5) years experience in database administration with a minimum of three (3) years of experience in Oracle database administration including Oracle 11G and/or Oracle 12c; and Oracle Enterprise Manager. Preferred Experience: Oracle Exadata; Oracle encryption, container databases, baseline plans; Unix shell scripting; and Db2 on Linux / UNIX, Sybase or SQL Server database administration. Knowledge, Skills and Abilities (KSAs): Knowledge of: The principals, practices and techniques of database design and development; database structures; and theories and current database technologies. Skill in: Administration of Oracle database environment including design and maintenance of Oracle databases; creating and tuning complex SQL, stored procedures, views, and triggers; monitoring and analyzing database performance; implementing database security standards; analyzing SQL access plans and indexing; and capacity assessment and planning. Ability to: Clearly communicate complex concepts, both verbally and in writing, to audiences with a wide range of backgrounds, experiences and expertise; devise solutions to problems; manage and coordinate multiple activities in different projects; design and administer database structures; work with software vendors, outsourced system staff and application developers to provide database support; and work within a team as well as independently to meet goals and deadlines.
