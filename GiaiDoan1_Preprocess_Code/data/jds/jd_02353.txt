The Campus Administrator helps coordinate campus operations, communications, facility-management as well as staff and congregational support. Position Responsibilities Primary office manager campus and campus team Assist Campus Pastor with General Operating budget Manage campus reception process (for walk-in’s, deliveries, visitors) Manage purchasing for facility and office supplies Manage campus upkeep and maintenance, working with Facilities and vendors to ensure facility upkeep, cleaning, and maintenance requests. Manage facility scheduling Supports and coordinates campus- projects, events as assigned by CP—e.g. campus communication, such as the campus newsletter, website updates, etc. Keep calendar updated with all events/programs—internal / external Manage campus processes between campus and central communications Primary owner of F1 database – ensure it remains updated and accurate Coordinate membership process (with central ministries) Assist with the flow of offerings & collected monies, including safe logistics & weekly count Recruit and lead volunteers for your ministry area, including safety team Other duties as assigned Position Qualifications Strong administrative and relational skills Highly organized, detail-oriented and flexible, particularly in dealing with multiple “moving parts” or priorities Strong communication skills – clear, direct and personable Demonstrated project management skills Proactive and reliable team player – e.g. will ask for help when needed and ask questions when needing clarity Experience in office management Experience in recruiting and managing volunteers Ability to manage multiple on-going projects Ability to multi-task with high energy, strong interpersonal skills, and positive attitude Ability to write and/or edit campus communication content Ability to recruit and lead volunteers Ability to use MS Excel, Outlook, Word, Fellowship One, Mail Chimp.
